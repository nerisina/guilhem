About me

Hello my name is Guilhem de Valicour and I am currently working at Alcatel-lucent in the Bell Labs Optical Technologies Research Domain. My main research interests are focused on design and characterization of optical devices, optical packet transport and switching for metropolitan and wireless backhaul networks, technology for access and RoF networks.

I have authored or co-authored more than 50 scientific papers in journals and international conferences, two book chapters and hold 13 patents. I received the 2011 "Best project" award from Alcatel-Lucent Bootcamp, the Marconi Young Scholar 2012 award and was finalist for the ParisTech PhD prize 2012. I am a IEEE member and member of the IEEE photonics society.

Research Highlight

Optoelectronics devices and photonic integration

I am fascinated with the possibility of handling the light through electronic devices. My work mainly focuses on the generation and the control of an optical signal through electro-to-optical conversion. Broad range of functionalities could be realized from a rather small set of key building blocks which I intent to model, design and characterize. By proper design and combination of these devices, advanced photonic integrated circuits could be realized pushing the limit of the photonic integration and following the path of the microelectronic world.

Toward next generation of optical network

High speed connectivity as well as network flexibility and high efficiency are needed for novel applications such as 3D television, video on demand and on line gaming. Innovative devices are required to reach high data bit rates that can fill the future demand and to handle these new traffic characteristics. I like to explore and develop new scenarios as well as realistic system demonstrations including photonics integrated devices combined with advanced modulation and detection techniques for next generation of datacenters, access and metropolitan networks.
